Common Baking Ingredients

Baking Soda

Baking soda (sodium bicarbonate) is a powerful leavening agent that produces carbon dioxide bubbles when combined with an acid.

Tip: Use with acidic ingredients like buttermilk or vinegar.

Function: Leavening | Substitutes: Baking powder + vinegar | Common in: Cookies, quick breads

Butter

Adds richness and tenderness to baked goods, contributing to leavening when creamed with sugar.

Tip: Keep butter cold for flaky pastries.

Function: Fat, Flavor | Types: Salted, Unsalted | Common in: Cookies, cakes

Eggs

Provide structure, richness, and moisture, helping bind ingredients together.

Tip: Use room temperature eggs for better mixing.

Function: Binding, Moisture | Substitutes: Applesauce, flax eggs | Common in: Cakes, muffins

Vanilla Extract

Enhances flavor and aroma in baked goods.

Tip: Use pure vanilla extract for the best flavor.

Function: Flavoring | Common in: Cakes, cookies

Milk

Adds moisture and richness, helping activate leavening agents.

Tip: Use whole milk for richer flavor.

Function: Moisture, Flavor | Common in: Cakes, breads
